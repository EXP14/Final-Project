Can AI produce "authentic" art?

To be able to answer this question, we must first delve into the definitions of both authentic and art. We define authenticty as the opposition of faking it. Walter Benjamin describes authenticity as "having a presence in time and space, and a unique existence in the place it happens to be." On the other hand, art is thought of as a physical manifestation of a thought or a feeling, with personal experience and cultural context used as inspiration. Others even argue that art is a medium often used to convey the full spectrum of human experiences.

By these definitions, some may argue that AI will never be considered authentic art. Because it requires test data, and no feeling or personal experience, AI generated art does not align with our notion of art.

On the other hand, there is evidence that AI is authentic art. While it creates something completely new and original based off of what already exists, it is not simply a mere mimic as some argue. Instead it is an entirely unique creation where the only difference lies in the tools used to create the piece.

Both sides of the argument offer valid points.
